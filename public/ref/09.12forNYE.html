Sort/Filter

First time on the site, you see the live/happening meetups for your company/employer in a chronological order from Monday to Sunday (always aka it doesn’t matter what day of the week it is today). As a particular meetup series (e.g. board games) happens weekly, this brings clarity aka a good overview of the meetups that are live/happening this month (every week is basically the same in a particular month, with minor variations).

Meetup filters with on/off IOSwitch toggles:

  1. “Show meetups looking for host” (toggle off means that you only see live meetups)
  1. Turned on -> off results in the not live gray background meetup cards to collapse/disappear

Add or turn on interests (header search bar with autofill)

Important Rule: whenever a new interest is added or toggle turned on/off, something visually changes in meetup cards (instant gratification for adding). Also, any meetup that is visible to you is also accessible to you (able to reserve a spot). You cannot access meetups that you cannot see - for them to become accessible/visible, you have to tell us more about your interests.

  1. Case 1: Before any interests are entered, you see the weekly meetups (deactivated with very light red) in chronological order from Monday to Sunday that
  1. Your company employees have access to (can see and reserve a spot to the meetup)
  2. You are eligible for according to your spoken languages
  1. Case 2: If the interest you add has a meetup it is adjacent to (e.g. interest ‘Worldly Discussions’ activates a meetup ‘Deeper Discussions’ that is currently live)
  1. Adjacent = cast the net as wide as possible
  2. Live = has a host and is scheduled (card background very light red)
  3. Activates = live meetup that is adjacent to your interest and has its IOSwitch toggle turned on (turns from a very light red to NoBail red). While the sorting will be chronological, NoBail brighter red makes the meetups stand out that are adjacent to your interests.
  1. Case 3: If the interest you add does not yet have a meetup it is adjacent to (e.g. interest ‘Worldly Discussions’ creates meetup ‘Reddit Offline’ that is not live).
  1. Not live = does not have a host yet (background light grey and with button ‘Try hosting?’)
  2. Deactivates =  live meetup that is adjacent to your interest and has its IOSwitch toggle turned off (turns from NoBail red to a very light red).
  3. Location of the card: if there is a day of the week without a meetup, the grey meetup card will appear on this day e.g. Monday has nothing then ‘Reddit Offline’ would take place on Monday (as an example). Keep things simple by setting it on the earliest day possible e.g. if Monday and Thursday have no meetup (live nor not live), set it on Monday. But once “Offline Reddit” is on Monday, the next (not live) grey meetup will be created onto Thursday.
  1. Case 4: Turn on toggle for existing interest ->
  1. Live meetups adjacent to that interest are activated (NoBail red)
  2. One meetup that is not live (AI generated template) appears at the top because perhaps you are willing to become a host by clicking on ‘Try hosting?’ button
  1. Case 5: Turn off toggle for existing interest ->
  1. Live meetups adjacent to that interest are deactivated (lighter red)
  2. The one meetup at the top that is not live simply collapses/disappears

Not employee edge case

Edge case: what if you are not an employee in a company that we have live meetups for?

Before adding interests: subcopy “Add interests to access meetups”

Not an employee in any company (logged in with personal email): grey not live meetups that are AI generated are visible with ‘Try hosting?’ button. With every interest you add, a new not live meetup is added to the top (ready to be hosted).
Same happens if you are an employee in some other company (that we have no live meetups for yet), but in a “For [company] employees” badge is added to the AI generated grey meetups (same badge as live meetups for particular company/companies employees)”

Not live meetups have ‘Save’ button just like live meetups. When the meetup goes live[ak], NoBail sends a notification to those that Saved the meetup.

Note: If we already have a suitable meetup generated for that same added interest by some previous user, no need to generate a new meetup (template). Show that same meetup, then this second user can also Save it and we can gauge demand.

If you reserve…

People outside your ‘social circle’ cannot see any of your profile until you have reserved your spot at meetup - not before. Only then will they see certain information about attendees that helps them make a decision whether they should reserve a spot.

Primary profile:

  1. To people outside your social circle, the primary profile is all hidden - name, employer, job, title, education, languages and social media accounts. They can be faded or greyed out to indicate that they will become visible after the meetup. So you are not identifiable before others meet you there.
  2. Visible to your ‘social circle’ as they want to see if you are going to a meetup so they would know to join.

Secondary profile:

  1. Visible to people outside your social circle: only Q&As in the ‘Public’ category
  2. Visible to people in your social circle: primary profile, Q&As in the ‘Public’ and ‘Social’  category are visible

Card designs

The design for the cards (venues, meetups, Q&As) should be similar but slightly different colors and sizes. In increasing size order:

  1. Questions (before answering - is the smallest as it doesn’t take that much info and we want as many questions visible as possible so the user would not have to scroll for browsing.
  1. The questions being presented should never end - I will put together top 200-300 that he will browse first, but over time they will also be sorted and additional AI generated ones inserted to prioritise. Automatic sorting happens according to:
  1. His primary profile (employer, background)
  2. Questions that the user has answered
  3. How he has answered the questions
  4. How he has categorised the questions answered. NoBail suggests similar questions to those answers that the user has move into social and public.
  1. Meetups - our goal is to have something happen every day for every larger employer of international employees aka 7 weekly meetups
  1. Active/live/visible on home page right away (before scrolling)
  1. Venues - largest “box” because there’s a visual (similar size to dailymeetup.ee) and we aim to work together with 5 venues at a time so they must be active/live/visible on home page right away (before scrolling)
